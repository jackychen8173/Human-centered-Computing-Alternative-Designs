# Alternative Designs

Interactive HTML mockups demonstrating alternative designs for addictive gaming features.

## 📱 Screens

### Current Design (Addictive - Red Theme)
- **Screen 1:** Guild & Friends - Scheduled raids, countdown timers, social pressure
- **Screen 2:** Raid Lobby - Waiting for 10 players, real-time coordination, can't leave
- **Screen 3:** Notifications - Urgent FOMO messaging, time-limited events, streak anxiety

### Alternative Designs (Ethical - Blue Theme)

**Primary Alternative: Async Guild System**
- **Screen 4:** Guild Quest Board - Async contributions, flexible 5-day deadlines
- **Screen 5:** Quest Contribution - Solo runs, choose difficulty, play when ready
- **Screen 6:** Solo Quest Run - Individual progress, save anytime, contributes to guild total

**Additional Alternatives (from report):**
- **Screen 7:** Transparent Matchmaking - See your MMR, understand match logic, no EOMM
- **Screen 8:** Reward Calendar - Predictable rewards, daily caps, no loot box gambling
- **Screen 9:** Active Breaks - Session stats, break reminders, no auto-queue trap

## 📚 Theoretical Framework

Based on research from:
- **Saini & Hodgins (2023)**: Taxonomy of addictive gaming structural features
- **Lim & Griffiths (2014)**: Flow, time dissociation, and addiction mechanisms
- **King et al. (2010)**: Structural characteristics and problematic gaming

## 🎯 Design Principles

**Current design optimizes for:**
- Maximum concurrent users (scheduled events)
- Engagement time (auto-queue, no stopping points)
- Social pressure (obligation, FOMO)
- Hidden manipulation (EOMM, variable rewards)

**Alternative designs optimize for:**
- Player temporal autonomy (flexible schedules)
- Transparent mechanics (no hidden algorithms)
- Natural stopping points (active breaks)
- Genuine connection without coercion
